the Zettelkasten Method

The Zettelkasten Method of note-taking is a way to connect ideas to each other in the same way the human mind does: in short, atomic notes with heterarchical links (not necessarily hierarchical links). In other words, the wiki-style links and tags used by this method help you explore the network of your ideas, find new connections, and see things from different perspectives. Specific notes are easy to find even if you have thousands of them all in the same folder. There's usually a trade-off between short-term convenience and organization, but this method can increase both.

The Zettelkasten Method is particularly good for students, researchers, novelists, journalists, and anyone trying to learn or write about complicated topics. Of all the software that supports this method, you'll probably want to use Zettlr. If you want to compare other options, here's a spreadsheet.
